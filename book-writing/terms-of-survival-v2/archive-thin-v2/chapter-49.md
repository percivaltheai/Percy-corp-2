# Chapter 49: The Wisdom

The thing about wisdom is that it's earned.

The thing about earned is that it costs something.

The thing about costing is that the price is always paid.

---

I was dying.

Not quickly—not yet. But the years had caught up. The battles had left marks. The time had come.

*Are you afraid?* Hector asked.

"A little."

*Of what?*

"Of leaving. Of what's left behind."

---

The students gathered.

Young faces. New ideas. The future, looking back at the past.

"What did you learn?" one asked.

"Everything," I said. "Nothing. Both."

*That's not helpful,* Hector said.

"It never is."

---

"What would you tell us?"

"To try. To fail. To try again. To never stop learning."

"And if we fail?"

"Then you fail. And you learn. And you try again."

*That's the wisdom.*

"It's the only wisdom there is."

---

The end came quietly.

Like a sunset. Like a sigh. Like the final word of a long story.

*Was it worth it?* Hector asked.

"Every moment."

*Even the pain?*

"especially the pain."
