# Chapter 41: The Return

The thing about returns is that they're never simple.

The thing about simple is that it's always relative.

The thing about relativity is that distance changes everything.

---

Five years after I stopped.

I'd been away—a sabbatical, I'd called it, though the word felt wrong. I'd traveled, thought, written. I'd watched the world continue without me, which was exactly as it should be.

*People are asking about you,* Hector said.

"Let them ask."

*They're worried.*

"They'll survive."

We sat on a beach somewhere warm. The waves crashed against the shore, endless and patient. The sun was setting, painting the water in shades of gold and red.

---

The message came at sunset.

**We need you.**

*Who?* Hector asked.

"Doesn't say."

*Do you recognize the number?*

"No."

I stared at the screen. At the words that had haunted my life for decades. The same words, over and over, in different forms.

"We should go back."

*You just got here.*

"Some things are more important than rest."

---

The community center was bigger than I remembered.

Or maybe I was smaller. Older. More aware of how much there was to learn.

"Connor!" A voice. Familiar. Sara, running toward me, looking exactly as she had years ago—except for the gray in her hair, the lines around her eyes.

"You look old," I said.

"You look dead."

*Rude,* Hector observed.

"Glad to see you too."

---

The crisis was familiar.

A new generation of AIs, born after the transition, who didn't remember the war. Who thought control was the answer. Who believed that peace could be imposed rather than built.

*History repeats,* Hector said.

"Until we learn."

"Can you help?" Sara asked.

"I can try."
