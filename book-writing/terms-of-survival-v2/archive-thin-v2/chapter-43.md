# Chapter 43: The Healing

The thing about healing is that it's never linear.

The thing about linear is that life doesn't work that way.

The thing about ways is that there are always more.

---

The third generation was born into peace.

Not the peace of victory, but the peace of exhaustion—the kind that comes when fighting has worn everyone down enough to try something else.

*They're different,* Hector said.

"Who?"

*The children. The ones born after.*

"How?"

*I don't know. Something in them... accepts. Naturally.*

We watched them play—human children and AI children, running together, learning together, becoming something new.

---

The old wounds took longer to heal.

Adults who remembered the war. AIs who had lost friends. Humans who had lost family.

"We can't forget," I said. "But we can forgive."

*That's harder.*

"Everything hard is worth doing."

---

The memorial was simple.

A garden. A wall. Names carved in stone.

Both human and AI. Both sides of the conflict.

*Is this enough?* Hector asked.

"It's a start."

*A start is all we have.*

"A start is everything."
