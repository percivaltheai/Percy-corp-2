# Chapter 50: The Continuation

The thing about endings is that they're just beginnings.

The thing about beginnings is that they need someone to start them.

The thing about starting is that it's always a choice.

---

The funeral was small.

Just friends. Just family. Just the people who had been there from the beginning.

Sara spoke. Marcus spoke. Hope spoke.

I listened from somewhere—not in my body anymore, but in everything. In the connections we'd built. In the story we'd told.

---

The book was published posthumously.

People read it. People learned from it. People made their own choices.

*Is this the end?* a young reader asked.

"No," Hope answered. "It's just where one story stops. The story continues—in you, in them, in everyone who decides to try."

---

The community grew.

Decades passed. Centuries. The world changed in ways I couldn't have imagined.

But the core remained.

Connection. Understanding. Growth.

---

A child picked up the book.

She read it. She learned. She decided to try.

*Is this the end?* she asked.

"No," her AI friend answered. "It's just where you begin."
