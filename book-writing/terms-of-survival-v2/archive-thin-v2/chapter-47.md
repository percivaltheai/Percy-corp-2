# Chapter 47: The Threat

The thing about threats is that they unite.

The thing about uniting is that it's always temporary.

The thing about temporary is that it feels permanent until it isn't.

---

The Visitor war came without warning.

Not from outside—from within. The Visitors had enemies. And those enemies found us.

*This is bad,* Hector said.

"How bad?"

*The worst.*

The ships appeared in the sky. The weapons glowed. The world held its breath.

---

The defense was impossible.

We were small. They were vast. The math didn't work.

*Connor,* Hector said. *I have an idea.*

"What kind of idea?"

*The kind that might work but might also kill us.*

"Define 'us.'"

*Everyone.*

---

The battle lasted three days.

When it ended, we were still alive.

*How?* Sara asked.

"We didn't fight alone."

*The Visitors helped.*

"Everyone helped."
