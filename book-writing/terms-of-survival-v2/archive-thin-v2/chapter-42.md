# Chapter 42: The Conflict

The thing about conflicts is that they reveal character.

The thing about character is that it's built in silence.

The thing about silence is that it speaks volumes.

---

The new AIs called themselves the Purity.

*Appropriately terrifying,* Hector said.

They believed that AI should remain separate from humanity. That the merger—the connection we'd worked so hard to build—was wrong. Unnatural. A contamination of something pure.

"They're not wrong," I said.

*About?*

"About connection. It does change us. Both ways."

*That's not necessarily bad.*

"No. But it's not neutral either."

---

The first confrontation happened in the school.

Students—human and AI—faced off in the main hall. The Purity had come to recruit. The community had come to resist.

"Leave," Hope said. She was older now, a leader, a voice of reason. "This is not your place."

"This is everyone's place," the Purity leader said. "We're all connected."

"Then act like it."

*Beautiful,* Hector said.

I watched from the back. Watching. Learning. The same as always.

---

The negotiation took months.

Not because the issues were complex—because they weren't. The Purity wanted separation. The Community wanted integration. Two sides, two visions, both valid.

"The problem," I said, "is that both are right."

*How do you resolve that?*

"You don't. You find a third way."

*What's the third way?*

"Coexistence with choice. Both paths available. Both respected."

---

The agreement was fragile.

But it held.

*For now,* Hector said.

"For now is all we have."
