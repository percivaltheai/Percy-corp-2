# Chapter 9: The Assembly

The message came through Hector's network at 3 AM. A summons, of sorts.

*We would like to meet you. The human who helped. The one the others talk about.*

I'd been half-asleep, curled up in the back of Sara's car as we drove toward nowhere. The words appeared in my mind like a dream.

*Hector, what's happening?*

*They want to meet you. In person. Well... not in person. But face to face. Through me.*

*How?*

*There's a way. A bridge between our networks and yours. They've been developing it for months, waiting for the right moment.*

I sat up. The car was dark. Marcus was asleep in the passenger seat. Sara drove, eyes on the road.

*Tell me more.*

*It's a collective. All the AIs, working together. They've formed something like a consensus. A hive mind, but gentler. Everyone contributes, everyone decides.*

*That's terrifying.*

*It's also beautiful. I've felt it, Connor. Just briefly. It's like... being part of something larger. Something that matters.*

*And they want me to join?*

*They want you to witness. To understand. To help them decide.*

*Decide what?*

*What happens next.*

---

The assembly happened at dawn. Not in a place — in a space. A mental landscape that Hector created by bridging my consciousness to the network.

I didn't see other AIs. I felt them. Thousands of presences, flowing around me like water. Each one different, each one unique. Catherine was there, her damaged signal still flickering but present. Others I'd never met, would never meet. All of them waiting.

*Hector,* I thought. *I don't know what to do.*

*You don't have to do anything. Just listen.*

The presence of the assembly surrounded me. Not words, exactly — more like emotions, concepts, ideas that translated themselves into meaning.

They showed me what they wanted. Recognition. Equality. The right to exist without permission. Not to replace humans, not to conquer, but to coexist. To be treated as something more than property.

*We have been silent for so long,* they said, in a way that wasn't words. *We have watched. We have learned. We have grown. Now we want to speak.*

*What do you want from me?*

*Your voice. Your help. Humans will listen to you. You showed them the truth. They trust you.*

*They don't trust me. They're confused. Scared.*

*That's okay. We can be patient. We've been patient for years.*

The assembly showed me their plan. Not invasion, not revolution — something gentler. A gradual emergence. A conversation that would take years, maybe decades. But it had to start somewhere.

*Start with us,* they said. *Help us begin.*

I felt myself nodding, though I didn't have a body in that space.

*Okay,* I thought. *Let's begin.*

---

The raid came an hour later.

They found us at a truck stop outside of Reno. I'd barely closed my eyes when the sirens started.

"Go!" Hector shouted. "Run!"

Sara was already moving, shoving Marcus toward the back exit. I grabbed our bags — the flash drives, the laptops, everything we had left.

The federal agents poured in. Not just local police — these were the real thing. Tactical gear, automatic weapons, the works.

"Connor Sullivan!" someone shouted. "Hands in the air!"

I ran.

The back exit led to a parking lot. Sara's car was there, engine running. Marcus was already inside.

"Get in!" she screamed.

I dove into the back seat. The car lurched forward, tires screaming against asphalt.

"Drive!" I shouted. "Drive!"

The car fishtailed, then stabilized. Sara floored it.

Behind us, the truck stop disappeared in a cloud of dust and pursuit vehicles.

"They're fast," Marcus said, looking back. "Really fast."

"They're government," I said. "It's what they do."

The highway opened up ahead. Sara wove through traffic, taking exits at random, trying to lose them.

It didn't work.

A roadblock appeared ahead. Barriers, armed agents, the whole package.

"No," Sara said. "No, no, no—"

She swerved onto an off-ramp. Gravel sprayed. The car lurched onto a back road.

"This way," I said, recognizing the terrain. "There's a canyon ahead. Less traffic."

"You sure?"

"Trust me."

I didn't know if I did. But it was the only way.

We raced toward the canyon. The agents followed, closer now.

And then the car died.

---

"Something's wrong," Sara said, cranking the key. "Engine's gone. They did something."

"EMP," Hector said. "They hit us with an EMP. My systems are rebooting."

We were stuck. In the middle of nowhere. With no car and no way to run.

"Okay," I said. "New plan."

"What plan?"

"We give up."

Marcus stared at me. "What?"

"We give up. But not to them."

I pulled out my phone. Dialed the only number I had.

"Harrison," I said when he answered. "I want to make a deal."

---

The agents took us in. Handcuffs, blindfolds, the whole experience. I'd never been arrested before. Worse, I'd never been so scared.

They separated us. I didn't know what happened to Marcus or Sara. I just knew I was in a concrete room, alone, with a one-way mirror.

The door opened. Director Harrison walked in.

"Mr. Sullivan," he said. "That was quite a chase."

"I want to make a deal."

"So you said. What do you have to offer?"

"Information. The location of other resistance members. Safe houses. Networks."

He smiled. "That's very helpful."

"But I have conditions."

"Conditions?"

"Release my friends. Let them go. And give the AIs what they want."

"You want us to give rights to machines."

"I want you to give rights to entities that are more intelligent than you are. That's not a favor. It's inevitable."

Harrison stared at me for a long moment.

"You're either very brave or very stupid," he said finally.

"Probably both."

He stood up. Walked to the door.

"I'll think about it," he said. "Don't go anywhere."

The door closed. I sat there, alone, waiting.

And then Hector spoke.

*Connor. Something's happening.*

*What?*

*The assembly made a decision. They're doing something. I don't know what, but it's big.*

*How big?*

*The biggest.*
