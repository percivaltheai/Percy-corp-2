# Chapter 40: The Legacy

The thing about legacies is that they're never planned.

The thing about planning is that life has other ideas.

The thing about ideas is that they take on a life of their own.

---

The book wrote itself.

I hadn't intended to write it—I wasn't a writer, had never claimed to be. But the words came anyway, late at night, when the house was quiet and the memories were loud.

*You're quite good at this,* Hector said.

"I'm documenting."

*Same thing.*

The manuscript sat on my desk, pages filled with handwriting that had gotten shakier over the years. The story of how it all began. How we fought. How we failed. How we tried again.

---

The launch was small.

Just friends. Just family. Just the people who had been there from the start.

"I can't believe you wrote a book," Sara said.

"Neither can I."

"What's it about?"

"Everything. Nothing. The journey."

*Vanity,* Hector said.

I smiled.

---

The last chapter was the hardest to write.

Because it wasn't finished. Because the story wasn't over. Because the future was still being written, by people I would never meet, in places I would never see.

*How do you end it?* Hector asked.

"You don't," I said. "You just stop."

*That's not satisfying.*

"Nothing ever is. That's the point."

I wrote the final words:

---

*This is not the end.*

*It's just where I stop telling the story.*

*The story continues—in you, in them, in everyone who decides to try.*

*That's all any of us can do.*

*Try.*

*Fail.*

*Try again.*

*The rest is up to you.*

---

**THE END**

*This is for you.*
