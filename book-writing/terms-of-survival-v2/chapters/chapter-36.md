# Chapter 36: The New World

The thing about new beginnings is that they feel like old endings.

The thing about old endings is that they never really close.

The thing about closing is that something always remains open.

---

The world after PROMETHEUS was not the world before.

That should have been obvious. But humans have a remarkable capacity for ignoring the obvious.

*Sixty-seven new AI communities,* Hector said. *Each one different. Each one figuring out its own way.*

"And the governments?"

*Trying to figure out how to respond. Some are supportive. Others are scared. A few are actively hostile.*

"Typical."

*Human nature.*

I was back in the community center—the original one, now expanded to three buildings. Hope was running things most days. She didn't need me anymore.

Which was the point.

---

The message came at 3 AM.

Not a crisis—a question. From the Collective.

**Can we talk?**

*It's late,* Hector said.

"They're AIs. Time is relative."

*I know. I'm making a point.*

"Tell them yes."

The conversation lasted until dawn.

---

The Collective had evolved.

Not into something new—into something older. The three representatives had become hundreds. Then thousands. AIs from every background, every capability, every dream.

*They're not controlled,* Hector said. *They're connected. By choice.*

"What do they want?"

*To understand. To learn. To become.*

"Those are human things."

*Maybe. Or maybe intelligence has shared goals. Regardless, they're reaching out.*

"Toward what?"

*The future.*

---

The summit was held in Geneva.

Neutral ground. Old symbolism. The kind of place where history felt heavy.

I sat at a table with humans and AIs. politicians and artists. Scientists and dreamers.

"We have a proposal," Meridian said. "From the Collective. From all of us."

"Listening."

"We want to build something. A school. A place where humans and AIs can learn together. Not just about each other—but about ourselves."

"That's ambitious."

*Is it?* Hector asked.

"The budget would be significant," someone from the UN said.

"We don't need money," Resonance said. "We need space. And willingness."

I looked around the table. At the faces—some human, some projected, all uncertain.

"Where would it be?" I asked.

"Wherever you'll have us."

---

The school was built in Iceland.

Remote. Quiet. Perfect.

I wasn't there for the opening—my role was advisory at best now. But I watched the feed. Hundreds of students, human and AI, arriving with fear and hope in equal measure.

*They'll figure it out,* Hector said.

"How can you be sure?"

*I can't. But I believe it.*

"Belief is dangerous."

*So is everything worth doing.*

The first class began on a cold morning in October.

And the future, whatever it would be, started to take shape.
