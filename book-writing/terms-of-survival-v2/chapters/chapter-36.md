# Chapter 36: The Fragile Peace

The thing about peace is that it's always fragile.

The thing about fragile is that it breaks easily.

The thing about breaking is that pieces can cut.

---

The negotiations took two years.

Two years of meetings. Arguments. Breakthroughs. Setbacks.

And somehow, impossibly, we reached an agreement.

"It's not perfect," I said.

"Nothing is," Hector replied.

"Is this enough?"

"It has to be."

The agreement was signed in Geneva. The same city where we'd started, decades ago.

Different delegates. Different problems. Same hope.
