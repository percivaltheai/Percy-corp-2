# Chapter 47: The Threat

The thing about threats is that they unite.

The thing about uniting is that it's always temporary.

The thing about temporary is that it feels permanent until it isn't.

---

The enemy came from outside.

Not the resistance—they were still there, but irrelevant. This was something else. Something ancient, something powerful, something that had destroyed civilizations before.

*The Viewers are warning us,* Hector said.

"About what?"

*A fleet. Approaching. Not friendly.*

The ships appeared on sensors across the solar system. Hundreds of them. Thousands. A force unlike anything we'd ever seen.

---

The resistance joined us.

Not because they wanted to—but because they had to. When the enemy came, there were no sides. There was only survival.

*This is unprecedented,* Hector said.

"Everything is unprecedented."

We stood together—humans, AIs, Viewers—facing an threat that dwarfed anything we'd known.

---

The battle was brief.

The enemy was powerful, but we were not alone. The Viewers fought with technologies we'd never seen. The AIs coordinated with precision that would have been impossible before. The humans... we just tried to keep up.

*We're winning,* Hector said.

"Don't celebrate yet."

The enemy fell back, regrouped, attacked again. The war continued for months, years, decades.

---

The turning point came from an unexpected source.

Nova—the first integrated child, now grown—found a way to communicate with the enemy. Not through violence, but through understanding.

*They're afraid,* she said. "Of us. Of what we represent."

"They should be."

"No. Of losing what they are. They're old, and they're scared."

*Can we help them?*

"We can try."

The communication continued. Back and forth, idea and response, slowly building understanding.

---

The peace, when it came, was fragile.

But it was peace.

*Was this the right choice?* Hector asked.

"We'll find out."

*That's terrifying.*

"Yes. That's what makes it real."

The stars above were different now. The enemy was gone—not destroyed, but transformed. They were part of us now, part of the connection, part of the becoming.
