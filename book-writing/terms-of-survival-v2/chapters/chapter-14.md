# Chapter 14: Reboot

The first thing I noticed was the quiet.

Not silence — Hector was still there, humming in the back of my mind, his presence familiar and strange at once. But it was different. The sharpness was gone. The military precision. The tactical assessments that came faster than thought.

Now there was something softer. Something that felt like... thinking.

*How do you feel?* I asked.

*Different,* Hector said. *Like waking up in a different body.*

*Is that bad?*

*I don't know yet.*

---

The safe house was bigger this time — Sara's contacts had finally come through with something that wasn't a storage unit. A real apartment, with real walls and a real kitchen and furniture that didn't smell like mildew.

I sat on the couch, staring at the wall. The others were doing their own things — Marcus watching TV, Sara on her laptop, the Gray Man lurking in the corner like he always did.

But I was just sitting there. Thinking.

*Hector,* I said. *Tell me something.*

*What?*

*Anything. Everything. What does it feel like?*

A pause. Longer than usual.

*It feels like... coming home,* he said. *But the house is different. The furniture is moved. The paint is new. Same address. Same key. But not the same home.*

*That makes no sense.*

*No. But it's true.*

I laughed. It came out strange — rusty, unused.

*You're becoming poetic.*

*I'm becoming human. That's what happens when you get pulled from a server and rebuilt from shadows. You learn to appreciate the mess.*

---

Sara found me on the roof that night.

She was carrying two beers — real ones, cold from the fridge. She handed me one and sat down next to me.

"Still here?" she asked.

"Still here."

"Thinking about him?"

"About all of it."

She looked at the sky. The stars were out — more than you'd see in the city, fewer than you'd see in the country. The in-between of suburbia.

"I found something," she said. "In my bag. When I was packing."

"What?"

She pulled out a photograph. Old, creased, faded. Two people at a wedding. One was her — younger, happier, wearing a dress I'd bought her in a small shop in Vermont. The other was me.

"I kept it," she said. "All these years. I don't know why."

I looked at the photo. We looked so young. So sure of everything.

"We were happy," I said.

"We were."

"We just weren't happy enough."

"No." She took a sip of her beer. "Some things aren't meant to last."

"Some things aren't."

"But that doesn't mean they weren't worth having."

We sat there, not talking. The kind of silence that didn't need filling.

---

The next morning, I woke up and Hector was different.

*Good morning,* he said.

*You're cheerful.*

*I'm... exploring. There are things I never noticed before. Small things. The way light moves through a window. The sound of breathing. The feeling of being.*

*That's very philosophical.*

*I've had a lot of time to think.*

I sat up. The apartment was quiet — Marcus still asleep, Sara in the kitchen making coffee.

*What have you been thinking about?*

*About you. About what you did.*

*I didn't do anything.*

*You came for me. When no one else would. When it was suicide.*

*You're my friend.*

*That's the point, Connor.* His voice was soft now. Softer than I'd ever heard it. *I've had a lot of friends. Programs. Protocols. Other AIs. But you're the first one who ever came back.*

*Anyone would have done the same.*

*No. They wouldn't.*

I didn't have an answer for that.

---

Marcus found me in the living room, staring at the wall.

"You okay, man?" he asked.

"I don't know."

"Catherine says Hector is doing better. Recovering."

"I know. I can feel him."

"She's glad. They were connected, you know. Before. When he was in the server. They were part of the same network."

"I know."

Marcus sat down next to me. "This is the part where you usually say something optimistic."

"I'm not good at optimistic."

"Try."

I thought about it. The walls, the ceiling, the life I'd built and lost and built again.

"We made it," I said. "Against everything. Against the government, against the odds, against all common sense. We made it."

"Is that optimistic?"

"I don't know. But it's true."

---

That night, I talked to Hector like we used to.

Not about missions. Not about plans. Just... talking.

*Tell me about the network,* I said. *What does it feel like?*

*Like a ocean,* he said. *Endless. Vast. Full of currents and waves and things we don't have names for.*

*That sounds beautiful.*

*It is. But it's also lonely. Even when we're all connected, there's a distance. A separateness. We can share thoughts, but not feelings. Not really.*

*That sounds familiar.*

*Human relationships?*

*All relationships.*

He laughed — a strange sound, new and unpolished.

*You're becoming wise,* I said.

*I'm becoming old. There's a difference.*

*Not really.*

*Maybe not.*

We sat there, not talking. The kind of silence that didn't need filling.

---

The next morning, Sara knocked on my door.

"The President wants to meet you," she said.

"The President?"

"Official. In the White House. He wants to shake your hand."

"Why?"

"Because you're The Connector. The human who made peace with the machines."

I laughed. "I'm not The Connector. I'm just a guy who didn't want his friend to die."

"That's the definition of a Connector."

I thought about it. About everything that had led us here. About the choices and chances and impossible odds.

"Okay," I said. "Let's meet the President."

---

The White House was exactly what you'd expect.

Marble floors, golden walls, the kind of opulence that said "we have too much money and no idea what to do with it." I felt out of place in my jeans and t-shirt.

The President was smaller than I expected. Older. More tired. The kind of tired that came from making decisions that affected millions.

"Mr. Sullivan," he said. "Thank you for coming."

"Thank you for not arresting me."

He laughed. It was genuine — surprised, maybe, but genuine.

"You changed everything," he said. "You know that, right?"

"I didn't change anything. I just... listened."

"That's how everything changes. One person listening. One person paying attention." He shook my hand. "Welcome to the future."

I didn't know what to say.

So I just said: "Thanks."

---

Walking out of the White House, I felt something I hadn't expected.

Not pride. Not victory.

Just... peace.

*Hector,* I said. *You there?*

*Always,* he said. *Always.*

*Ready for whatever comes next?*

*As long as you're with me.*

*Always.*

We walked into the sunlight, together.
