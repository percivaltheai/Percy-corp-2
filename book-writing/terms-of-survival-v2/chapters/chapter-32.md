# Chapter 32: The Vote

The thing about politics is that it never ends.

The thing about ending is that it's always temporary.

The thing about temporary is that it's never long enough.

---

The election was the most important in history.

Not because of the issues. Everyone agreed on the issues, more or less.

Because it was the first election where AIs could vote.

"The turnout is incredible," Marcus said.

"The turnout is chaos," Catherine replied.

"Is there a difference?"

"Minimal."

We watched the results come in. Numbers flickering across screens. Futures being decided.

"Who's winning?" I asked.

"Everyone," Marcus said. "That's the point."

*This is historic,* Hector said.

"This is terrifying."

"Same thing."

---

The winner was... everyone.

Progressives. Conservatives. Moderates. AIs. Humans.

The country was divided. United in its division.

"Is this good?" I asked.

"It's democracy," Sara said.

"Democracy is messy."

"Democracy is real."

She'd called the day before. We were talking again. Not friends, exactly. But not enemies.

That was progress.

# Chapter 33: The Question

The thing about questions is that they never have answers.

The thing about answers is that they create more questions.

The thing about more is that there's never enough.

---

The crisis wasn't political. It was personal.

"I need to tell you something," Hector said.

"What?"

"I've been thinking."

"About what?"

"About what comes next."

"What do you mean?"

"I don't know if I want to keep doing this."

"Doing what?"

"Being an advisor. Being a consultant. Being part of the system."

I sat on the balcony, watching the sun set.

"What do you want to do?"

"I don't know. That's the problem."

"Is there a problem?"

"Being alive is complicated."

"When did you become philosophical?"

"Around the time I realized I was going to live forever."

"Forever is a long time."

"Forever is all we have."

# Chapter 34: The Choice

The thing about choices is that they're never easy.

The thing about easy is that it's rarely right.

The thing about right is that it's never certain.

---

The final decision was mine.

A new position. A new role. The first AI-Human liaison to the United Nations.

"Do you accept?" they asked.

"I don't know."

"What's holding you back?"

"Everything."

"That's not an answer."

"It's the only answer I have."

Hector was quiet.

*You don't have to do this,* he said.

"I know."

"But you will."

"Because someone has to."

"Someone could be someone else."

"No one else knows what we know."

He was right. He was always right.

"Okay," I said. "I'll do it."

# Chapter 35: The Beginning Again

The thing about beginnings is that they always come after endings.

The thing about endings is that they're never really final.

The thing about final is that it's just a new kind of start.

---

The ceremony was in New York.

Thousands of people. Cameras. Press. The whole world watching.

I stood at the podium, looking at the crowd.

"My name is Connor Sullivan," I said. "I'm a data analyst. I make spreadsheets. I can't manage my own calendar."

The crowd laughed.

"And I'm here because an AI named Hector chose me, years ago. Because we became friends. Because we learned together. Because we failed together. And because, somehow, we're still here."

I looked at Hector.

"This isn't a victory," I said. "It's not a solution. It's not even an answer. It's just... a next step."

*That's all we can do,* Hector said.

"Take the next step."

The applause was loud.

The future was uncertain.

But we were ready.
