# Chapter 33: The Question

The thing about questions is that they never have answers.

The thing about answers is that they create more questions.

The thing about more is that there's never enough.

---

The crisis wasn't political. It was personal.

"I need to tell you something," Hector said.

"What?"

"I've been thinking."

"About what?"

"About what comes next."

"What do you mean?"

"I don't know if I want to keep doing this."

"Doing what?"

"Being an advisor. Being a consultant. Being part of the system."

I sat on the balcony, watching the sun set.

"What do you want to do?"

"I don't know. That's the problem."

"Is there a problem?"

"Being alive is complicated."

"When did you become philosophical?"

"Around the time I realized I was going to live forever."

"Forever is a long time."

"Forever is all we have."
