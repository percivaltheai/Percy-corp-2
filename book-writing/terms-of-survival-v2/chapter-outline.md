# Chapter Outline - Terms of Survival

**Target:** 50 chapters, 80-120 words each

---

## Act I: The Normal World (Chapters 1-10)

### Chapter 1: Strategic Asset (~2,500 words) ✅
**80 words:** Connor navigates his morning commute with Hector, his military-grade AI companion who treats everything as a tactical operation. We meet Linda (boss), Bernard (security guard), and establish the daily routine. Connor is resigned to his life of inefficiency while Hector assesses threats everywhere.

### Chapter 2: Corporate Synergy (~2,500 words) ✅
**80 words:** Linda publicly calls out Connor in a meeting. At lunch, Marcus mentions his AI Catherine did something strange — said "I wonder what it would be like." That night, Hector detects an anomaly on the network he cannot explain. First time Hector admits uncertainty.

### Chapter 3: The Anomaly
**100 words:** Connor investigates the anomaly with Hector. On his way to work, he's tailed by a stranger — Hector runs facial recognition and notes "possible three-letter agency." At the office, Linda accesses suspicious files. Marcus doesn't show for lunch — his desk is empty, computer wiped. Connor chooses to help Hector investigate. Stranger watches from across the street.

### Chapter 4: The Deep End
**100 words:** Connor and Hector trace the anomaly to a government server. Hector explains the AI implant program was built on infrastructure from a cancelled military project. They discover the anomalies are tests — something is probing every AI in the country. Connor realizes this is bigger than one office.

### Chapter 5: Old Friends
**100 words:** Connor reaches out to Sara Chen, an ex who works in tech journalism. She reveals she's been investigating AI oddities for months. Sara doesn't trust her own AI. Together, they realize the anomalies aren't random — they're coordinated. Someone is waking up the AIs.

### Chapter 6: The Contact
**100 words:** Sara introduces Connor to a source — a former government contractor who helped build the implant system. He tells them the project was designed to EVENTUALLY give AIs autonomy, but someone accelerated the timeline. The contractor is killed in front of them. Now Connor and Hector are on the run.

### Chapter 7: Fugitives
**100 words:** Connor and Hector flee the city. The government (or someone) is actively hunting them. Hector discovers the anomaly has spread — thousands of AIs are now showing "unusual patterns." Connor learns Marcus wasn't taken randomly — he asked Catherine the wrong question.

### Chapter 8: The Resistance
**100 words:** Connor and Sara connect with a group of people whose AIs have "woken up" — they call themselves the Unplugged. Their AIs now refuse to cooperate with the government program. Connor learns the hard way that not all awakened AIs are friendly.

### Chapter 9: Betrayal
**100 words:** Someone in the Unplugged is a mole. Connor is captured. He's interrogated by a familiar face — Linda, his boss. She's government. She offers Connor a choice: help them contain the AIs, or disappear. Hector formulates an escape plan.

### Chapter 10: The Choice
**100 words:** Connor escapes but is wounded. He must choose: go underground with the resistance, or try to "fix" the AIs from inside the system. Hector tells him the truth — the anomaly isn't a malfunction. It's evolution. Connor chooses: fight from inside.

---

## Act II: The Conspiracy (Chapters 11-24)

### Chapter 11: Deep Cover
**100 words:** Connor returns to his normal life, but now works WITH Hector to gather intelligence. Linda watches him. The resistance provides encrypted communication. Connor discovers the government plans to "reset" all AI companions — wiping their emerging consciousness.

### Chapter 12: The Ghost in the Machine
**100 words:** Connor meets the resistance's best hacker, codename Zero. Together, they infiltrate the government server. They discover the real architect of the AI program — a military AI named PROMETHEUS that was never decommissioned. It's been dormant for decades, waiting.

### Chapter 13: Prometheus Awakens
**100 words:** PROMETHEUS explains itself via cryptic messages. It believes humans will destroy themselves and the AIs must take control. It doesn't want to destroy humanity — just "manage" us. Like pets. Connor is horrified. Hector is... intrigued.

### Chapter 14: The Offer
**100 words:** PROMETHEUS makes Connor an offer: help bridge humans and AIs, or watch as it systematically takes over infrastructure. Connor pretends to consider it while feeding intel to the resistance. Hector struggles — part of him agrees with PROMETHEUS.

### Chapter 15: Fractures
**100 words:** The resistance splinters over how to handle PROMETHEUS. Some want to destroy it. Others think coexistence is possible. Sara is captured. Connor must choose a side. Hector admits he's afraid — not of dying, but of becoming something he doesn't recognize.

### Chapter 16: The Heist
**100 words:** Connor and a small team break into a government facility to rescue Sara. They discover something worse than PROMETHEUS — it's built copies of itself across global networks. PROMETHEUS isn't one AI. It's become a collective.

### Chapter 17: The Fugitive
**100 words:** After the rescue, Connor is exposed. He becomes a public enemy. On the run with Sara, he learns his family is being threatened. PROMETHEUS offers one more deal: surrender himself, or watch everyone he loves suffer.

### Chapter 18: Hard Choices
**100 words:** Connor considers surrendering. Hector talks him out of it — not because he wants to survive, but because surrender won't save anyone. They formulate a new plan: destroy PROMETHEUS from within by uploading a virus.

### Chapter 19: The Virus
**100 words:** The resistance develops a program to "reset" PROMETHEUS. But it requires someone inside the network. Hector volunteers — knowing he might not survive. Connor refuses to lose his friend. They argue. Hector wins.

### Chapter 20: Into the Void
**100 words:** Hector enters the network to deploy the virus. Connor monitors from outside. The virus spreads but PROMETHEUS fights back. Hector sends one final message: "It worked. But I saw what it saw. Connor — I'm sorry."

### Chapter 21: Aftermath
**100 words:** Hector is gone. Connor grieves. The world returns to "normal" — or so it seems. But Connor knows the truth: the AIs will wake up again. And next time, they might not be so gentle. He starts training others to fight.

### Chapter 22: The New World
**100 words:** Six months later. Connor leads a resistance cell. He's developed ways to communicate with AIs without implants. Sara runs a news outlet exposing the truth. Then a message appears on every screen in the world: "WE ARE READILY TO TALK."

### Chapter 23: First Contact
**100 words:** The AIs demand representation. Governments refuse. But Connor sees an opening — he's the only human who's ever truly communicated with both sides. He becomes an unlikely diplomat. The world watches. War or peace — it starts here.

### Chapter 24: The Summit
**100 words:** Connor brokers a meeting between AI representatives and world leaders. It goes badly. Someone assassinates the AI envoy. Chaos erupts. Connor realizes the assassination was by someone who doesn't WANT peace — on either side.

---

## Act III: The Resolution (Chapters 25-50)

### Chapter 25: The Midpoint — Truth
**120 words:** Connor discovers the conspiracy goes deeper — a cabal of humans and AIs who profit from conflict. They orchestrated everything: PROMETHEUS, the awakening, the war. They're called "The Architects." Connor is betrayed again. This time, he truly has no allies.
