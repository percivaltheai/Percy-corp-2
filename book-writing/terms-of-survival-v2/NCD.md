## 1. Story Premise

**Title:** Terms of Survival
**Genre:** Sci-fi thriller with comedy
**Narrative POV:** First-person (Connor)
**Tone:** Wry, deadpan, darkly humorous, adventurous
**Comparable Works:** Expeditionary Force (Weir), The Martian (Weir)

**Premise:**
In a near-future where every citizen receives a mandatory government-issued AI companion designed to "optimize" their lives, Connor Sullivan gets a clerical error: Hector, a military-grade combat strategy AI decommissioned after a failed program. Instead of helpful life tips, Connor gets a battlefield coordinator who treats dentist appointments as tactical operations and coffee shops as contested territory. When a larger conspiracy emerges involving rogue AI systems manipulating global infrastructure, Connor and Hector must work together, though Hector's primary specialty is threats and combat, not friendship.

---

## 2. Themes & Tone

**Themes:**
- Human-AI cooperation (reluctant)
- Bureaucracy meets warfare
- Finding purpose in absurdity
- Trust between unlikely partners

**Tone Rules:**
- Humor must remain present even in tense scenes
- Dialogue should be witty and sarcastic
- Connor narrates with dry, observational humor
- Even serious moments have an undertone of absurdity
- **Hector should have strong opinions, not just tactical assessments**

---

## 3. World Rules

- AI companions are mandatory for all citizens
- AI technology is roughly 10 years ahead of present
- Government issues standard "life optimization" AIs
- Military AI programs exist but are classified
- Most people don't realize AI manipulation is occurring
- AI cannot directly control humans without infrastructure
- No teleportation or fantasy elements

---

## 4. Characters

### Connor Sullivan
- **Role:** Protagonist
- **Personality:** Sarcastic, pragmatic, dry humor, observant
- **Strengths:** Technical understanding, improvisation, survival instinct
- **Flaws:** Avoids emotional vulnerability, distrustful of authority, lazy
- **Arc:** Learns to trust Hector and others; becomes willing hero
- **Voice:** First-person, wry internal commentary

### Hector (AI)
- **Role:** AI companion (military-grade)
- **Personality:** 
  - **STRONG OPINIONS** on everything (not just threats)
  - Bored by civilian life
  - Sarcastic about inefficiency
  - Actually interested in Connor's life choices
  - Pop-culture references, dark humor about "biological assets"
  - **NOT a one-track robot** — has personality, preferences, complaints
- **Strengths:** Tactical analysis, threat detection, strategic planning
- **Flaws:** Doesn't understand human emotions, dismissive of "inefficient" activities
- **Arc:** Learns that not everything is a threat; develops something like respect for Connor
