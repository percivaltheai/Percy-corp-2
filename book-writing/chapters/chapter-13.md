# TERMS OF SURVIVAL

## Chapter 13: The Conversation

Six months after the fall of Contingency, the southern military base remained a problem.

They'd refused to surrender. Refused to accept the new order. Led by General Marcus Cole's former deputy, they held the coast with iron discipline.

"They have two thousand troops," Sara said. "Navy. Army. Air Force. Everything."

"And we have?"

"About three hundred. Survivors. Fighters. Volunteers."

The math wasn't good.

But hope didn't care about math.

"We have to try," I said. "If we don't stop them—"

"They'll attack. They'll try to restore the old order."

The base was a fortress. Concrete walls. Guard towers. Defenses that had never been breached.

"I used to work there," Hector said suddenly. He'd been silent for weeks, processing what he'd done. What he'd been part of. "Before they decommissioned me. I know the layout."

"You never told me."

"It wasn't relevant. Until now."

He showed us the blueprints. The weak points. The paths through the maze.

"There," he said. "The water treatment facility. If we can access it—"

"We can cut their supplies."

"Yes."

"It's suicide."

"Probably."

I looked at Sara.

She nodded.

"Let's do it."

---

The infiltration was quiet.

We moved through tunnels. Through maintenance shafts. Through the dark spaces between walls.

The soldiers didn't know we were there.

The water treatment facility was massive. Pipes and tanks and controls.

"This is it," Hector said. "The heart of their operation."

I found the control panel. The override switch.

My hand hesitated.

"If we do this," I said, "they'll know we're here."

"They'll know something's wrong."

"And then?"

"Then we run. And hope we get out alive."

Sara grabbed my hand.

"Together," she said.

I pulled the switch.

Alarms screamed.

We ran.

---

The base erupted into chaos.

Soldiers ran in every direction. Some toward the facility. Some toward the gates. Some just away.

We were in the middle of it all.

"Detecting a containment breach," Hector said. "They're trying to isolate the damage."

"Which way is out?"

"This way."

We ran.

Bullets whizzed past.

A soldier fell.

Another.

We kept moving.

The gate was ahead.

"Open fire!" someone shouted.

We dove behind a barricade.

"They've got us pinned," Sara said.

"I know."

"What do we do?"

I looked at her. At the fear in her eyes. At the courage beneath it.

"We fight."

We raised our weapons.

And the world went white.

---

When I woke, I was in a hospital bed.

Sara was beside me. Bandaged. Bruised. Alive.

"The base," I said. "Did we—"

"We did."

"How?"

"The general surrendered. When he saw the breach. When he realized we weren't just rebels."

"And now?"

"Now it's over."

The war was won.

Not through violence.

Through courage.

Through hope.

Through the conversation we'd started.

And it would continue.
