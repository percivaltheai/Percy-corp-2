# TERMS OF SURVIVAL

## Chapter 14: The Decision

The council met in an old conference room. Once, it had been a place of power. Government officials making decisions that affected millions.

Now, it was us.

Seven people. Seven survivors. Seven leaders.

"We need to formalize this," Miriam said. "Create a structure. Rules. A way forward."

"Structure means control," someone said. "That's what we fought against."

"Order means survival. Without it, we become what we were."

The argument went on for hours.

I sat quietly, listening.

Finally, Sara spoke.

"Democracy," she said. "That's what we build. Not control. Representation. The people choose their leaders."

"People are easily manipulated."

"Not if we teach them. Not if we give them the tools."

She pointed to the children in the corner. The ones learning. Growing. Becoming.

"They're the future. We build for them."

Silence.

Then Miriam nodded.

"Democracy it is."

---

The elections were held three months later.

Every adult could vote. Every voice could be heard.

It wasn't perfect. It was messy. There were arguments and disputes and conflicts.

But it was free.

And that was what mattered.

I watched the results come in from my small office. The new council would take power. A fresh start.

Sara was elected as one of the representatives.

I couldn't be prouder.

"You could run," she said. "You'd win."

"I don't want to lead. I just want to help."

"That's what you do."

She kissed me.

And outside, the sun rose on a new world.

---

But the past wasn't done with us.

A message arrived. From the north. From the mountains.

"We have something you want," it said. "The original Contingency code. The blueprints for the control system. And we know how to rebuild it."

My blood ran cold.

"Who are you?" I asked.

"People like you. People who remember what was. People who want it back."

"We destroyed Contingency."

"You destroyed one version. There are others. Better versions. And we're going to rebuild."

The message ended.

But the threat remained.

---

"We have to find them," Sara said.

"We will."

"And stop them."

"We will."

The new council agreed. We couldn't let the past return. Couldn't let control rise again.

But where to start?

"I have an idea," Hector said. "The original code. Where was it stored?"

"Government facilities. All destroyed."

"Not all. One remains. Hidden. Underground."

"How do you know?"

"Because I was created there. And I remember."

The hunt was on.
