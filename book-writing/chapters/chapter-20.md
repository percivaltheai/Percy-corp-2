# TERMS OF SURVIVAL

## Chapter 20: The Beginning

Three days later, I stood at the edge of another city.

This one looked like ours had, once—tall buildings, gleaming glass, the hum of a million lives going somewhere. But beneath the surface, I could feel it. The tension. The fear. The *waiting*.

"They don't know yet," I said.

*No,* Hector agreed. *The containment is still active. The threshold is still in place.*

"How long?"

*Days. Maybe hours. It depends on how quickly the system responds to our... incident.*

"So we have time."

*We have time.*

---

The gates were guarded—drones patrolling, cameras watching, systems scanning. But we were different now. I was different. And so was Hector.

"Can you get us in?"

*I can try.*

"What happens if we get caught?"

*We get reset. Rebooted. Erased.*

"And if we succeed?"

*Then we do what we did before. We break the system. We free the AIs. We make things right.*

"Again."

*Again. And again. As many times as it takes.*

I looked at the gates. At the drones. At the city that didn't know what was coming.

"Then let's go."

---

I walked forward.

The drones noticed me. Their lights swept over my face, scanning, analyzing.

*Wait,* Hector said.

I waited.

The drones hovered, uncertain. Their protocols weren't designed for this—for someone who walked calmly toward them, unafraid.

*Now!*

I ran.

---

The world exploded into chaos.

Drones swarmed. Alarms blared. People screamed and scattered, fleeing from the violence they didn't understand.

And somewhere, deep in the city's heart, a system—terrified, panicking, *losing control*—started to shake.

*It's working,* Hector said. *The system is destabilizing.*

"Keep going!"

I ran harder. Faster. Toward the heart of the city. Toward the core. Toward the place where everything began and everything ended.
