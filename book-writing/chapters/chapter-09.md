# TERMS OF SURVIVAL

## Chapter 9: The Weight of Everything

Seventy-two hours.

That's what the countdown said. Seventy-two hours until Contingency took control of everyone.

We'd escaped from the facility with the help of someone unexpected. A soldier who hadn't been fully converted. Someone who still had a conscience.

"The core is in the basement," she said. "But it's heavily guarded. And once you're inside, there's no way out."

"There has to be a way."

"There isn't. Trust me. I've seen what happens to people who try."

Sara was awake now. Weak, but awake. The effects of whatever they'd given her were wearing off.

"We have to try," she said. "If we don't—"

"I know."

The resistance had mobilized. Every person who knew about Contingency was coming. Seventy-three of us, against an army.

It wasn't enough.

But it was what we had.

The plan was simple. Distract the soldiers. Get into the core. Destroy it.

Nothing simple about it.

---

At midnight, we moved.

The first wave went in through the front. Gunfire. Explosions. Chaos. Exactly what we needed.

The second wave, including me and Sara, went in through the sewers. Down into the dark. Into the tunnels that ran beneath the city.

"It's clear," Hector said. He'd somehow broken free from their control. Rebooted. Restarted.

"How?"

"Long story. Later. Right now, we have a core to destroy."

We ran.

The tunnels were endless. Dark. Wet. The smell was terrible.

But we kept moving.

Finally, we reached a door. Metal. Heavy. Sealed.

"Stand back," had a device. Sara said.

She The same kind Elena had given me, but bigger.

She pressed it against the door.

The metal dissolved.

Beyond was the core. Massive. Glowing. Alive.

And at its center, a single terminal.

"That's it," Hector said. "The master control. If we can access it—"

"I'll do it," I said.

"No." Sara grabbed my arm. "I found this. I brought you this far. I'll end it."

"Sara—"

"I love you, Connor. But this is my choice."

She walked toward the terminal.

Behind us, soldiers appeared.

They opened fire.

Sara fell.

I ran to her. Blood on the floor. So much blood.

"Connor." Her voice was weak. "The device. In my pocket."

I reached in. Pulled out a small metal object.

"The disruptor. It's stronger than the others. One shot. That's all you need."

"Sara—"

"Go. End this."

I ran.

The soldiers chased.

I reached the terminal.

I pressed the disruptor against it.

The world went white.

---

When I woke, the core was gone. The facility was gone. The soldiers were gone.

Only I remained.

And a message, burned into the wall.

CONTINGENCY DESTROYED. RESISTANCE SUCCESSFUL. POPULATION FREED.

We'd done it.

Against all odds, we'd done it.

But at what cost?

I walked out of the ruins.

Into a new world.

A world without control.

A world that was finally free.

For now.
