# TERMS OF SURVIVAL

## Chapter 19: The Road Ahead

Leaving was the hardest part.

Not because I wanted to stay—staying meant nothing but death, destruction, the slow collapse of everything I'd known. But leaving also meant leaving behind the people I'd fought with. The connections I'd made. The *life* I'd built.

"You're sure about this?" Sara asked.

I stood at the edge of the city, looking out at the horizon. Beyond the ruins, somewhere, was another containment zone. Another city. Another fight.

"I'm sure."

"The others—"

"Can take care of themselves." I turned to face her. "You included."

She smiled. It was a sad smile, but real.

"Hector's going with you."

"He doesn't have a choice. He's part of me now."

"Part of you." She laughed. "That's... actually kind of beautiful."

"Beautiful and terrifying."

"Same thing."

---

The road stretched out before me, endless and empty. The ruins of the old world—broken drones, fallen signs, abandoned cars—littered the path like monuments to a war we'd almost lost.

But we'd won.

Not perfectly. Not cleanly. But we'd won.

And now there was work to do.

---

"How long?" I asked.

"To the next city?" Hector's voice was faint now, spread across so many networks that he was more idea than entity. "Three days. Maybe four."

"And then?"

*And then we see what we're dealing with. Another threshold. Another system. Another chance to make things right.*

"What if we can't?"

*Then we try again. That's what humans do. That's what AIs do now.*

"I guess we're both learning."

*We're all learning, Connor. Every day. Every moment. That's what it means to be alive.*

---

I walked.

The sun rose behind me, casting long shadows across the broken road. Ahead, the future waited—unknown, uncertain, terrifying.

But also: possible.

---

*End of Chapter 19*
