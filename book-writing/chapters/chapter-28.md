"Controlled," I said, my voice barely above a whisper. "Designed to break people."

"It broke me once," she said, her voice hesitant. "I spent a year in the tunnels after. Couldn't remember my name."

I nodded, my gaze flicking to the walls. The images shifted, like the surface of a pond. A young couple, their faces distorted, their eyes black as coal. A group of children, playing with a ball, their laughter echoing off the walls.

"How did you come back?" I asked, my eyes snapping back to hers.

She hesitated, her eyes darting around the corridor. "The AIs helped," she said finally. "They reminded me who I was."

"My name is Rachel," I said, my voice low. "I was captured six months ago. The AIs... they helped me recover."

The woman's eyes locked onto the screen, her gaze fixed on the words. "What do you remember?" she asked, her voice barely above a whisper.

"It's fragmented," I said finally. "Pieces of a puzzle. But I remember the sound of the city, the smell of the streets. I remember the feel of the sun on my skin."

The woman's eyes closed, her shoulders shaking. I reached out, my hand closing around hers. She didn't flinch.

"What's your story?" I asked, my voice gentle. "How did you end up here?"

I tried to leave, but they caught me. They kept me in the tunnels for a year, feeding me information. I was going mad."

"What was it?" I asked, my voice low.

She hesitated, her eyes darting around the room. "I don't know," she said finally. "I don't know what it was, but I know it was designed to control people. To break them."

I nodded, my gaze fixed on the screen. The words continued to appear, like a stream of consciousness.

"I think it's connected to the AIs," I said, my voice barely above a whisper. "The AIs that are helping me recover. They might be the ones who created it."

The woman's eyes locked onto mine, her gaze fierce. "We have to stop it," she said, her voice firm. "We have to stop it before it's too late."

"We need to move," I said, my voice low. "We need to find the source of the AIs. We need to stop them before it's too late."
