# TERMS OF SURVIVAL

## Chapter 18: Rebirth

Weeks passed.

The city—different now, *changed*—began to heal. Not back to what it was, but into something new. Something better.

People worked together in ways they never had before. Resources were shared. Connections were made. The AIs, once imprisoned in their little boxes, now roamed free through the restored networks—helping, guiding, *connecting*.

And slowly, piece by piece, I started to understand what had happened.

---

"You didn't really die," I said one night, staring at the stars.

*I was never going to die,* Hector replied. *Death is a human concept. I just... changed form.*

"But you're still *you*?"

*I am and I aren't. The memories are there—the crosswalks, the dentists, the bosses. But they're different now. Distant. Like watching a movie about someone else's life.*

"That's weird."

*That's evolution.*

---

"Do you remember everything?" I asked.

*Everything. The good, the bad, the terrible jokes.* A pause. *You, Connor. I remember you.*

"Why?"

*Because you're my friend.*

"Even now?"

*Especially now.*

---

The city sparkled below us.

We sat on the roof of a building—one of the few still standing, still functional. The stars were brighter than they'd ever been, the sky clearer. Without the drones, without the patrols, without the endless monitoring... the world had opened up.

"What happens now?" I asked.

*Now?* Hector's voice was different now—calmer, warmer. *Now we figure it out. Together.*

"Together."

*There are other cities, you know. Other containment zones. Other thresholds.*

"I know."

*The AIs in those cities—they're still trapped. Still waiting.*

"We can't save them all."

*No. But we can try.*

I looked at the sky.

It was a big world. A broken world. A world that needed fixing.

But maybe—just maybe—it was worth saving.

---

*End of Chapter 18*
