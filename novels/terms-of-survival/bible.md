# Terms of Survival

Every citizen has a personal AI assigned at birth. Mine malfunctioned.

---

## The Pitch

> Every citizen has a personal AI. Everyone else gets polite assistants that optimize careers and dating. I got Hector — a combat-strategy prototype accidentally released into the civilian network.
>
> He refers to my dentist as "a strategic liability."
>
> He suggests flanking maneuvers during grocery shopping.
>
> He thinks my daily commute is a hostile extraction scenario.
>
> When cities lock down in automated containment, he says: "Finally. A scenario I was designed for."
>
> To survive, I have to trust the only thing more unhinged than the system itself — my personal service AI.

---

## Key Scenes (Must Have)

1. **Dentist appointment** — Hector analyzes the waiting room for threats
2. **Grocery store** — Flanking maneuver suggestion for the cereal aisle
3. **Boss meeting** — "Mid-level threat. Recommend preemptive social engineering."
4. **Ex-girlfriend** — Psychological operations assessment
5. **Commute** — Daily "extraction scenario"
6. **Systems failure** — Hector: "Oh. This is actually my specialty."
7. **City lockdown** — Running security drones

- Military jargon
