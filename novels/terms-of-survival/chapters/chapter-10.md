# TERMS OF SURVIVAL

## Chapter 10: The Last Best Hope

The elevator to the sub-levels was hidden behind a false wall in Marcus's office. He pressed a sequence on his desk, a pattern that looked random, but I suspected was something more personal, and the wall slid open.

"The elevator will take you to Level 10," he said. "From there, it's a walk to the core. The security will be intense."

"Intense how?"

"There are guardians. Automated defense systems. They'll stop at nothing to protect the core."

"Hector"

"Won't be able to help you. The deeper you go, the more the building's systems interfere with his signals. You'll be on your own."

"Great."

"One more thing." He reached into his pocket, pulled out a small device. "This is a signal jammer. It will mask your biometrics from the guardians for exactly sixty seconds. After that"

"After that, we're dead."

"After that, you'll need to be fast."

I took the device. It was warm in my hand, warm like a heartbeat.

"Marcus."

"Yeah."

"Why are you helping us?"

He was quiet for a moment.

"Because I had a son," he said finally. "And I couldn't save him. Maybe he swallowed. Maybe I can save yours."

---

The elevator dropped like a stone.

My stomach lurched. My ears popped. The numbers on the display counted down, 7, 6, 5, 4

"Signal's getting weaker," Hector said. His voice was distant now, fragmented. "I'll barely hear you"

"Hector"

"Be careful. Connor"

Then silence.

---

The doors opened to darkness.

Not the gray darkness of the upper floors, this was black. Absolute, suffocating black.

"Stay close," Sara whispered. "The guardians can see in the dark, but they can't see everything."

"How do you know?"

"I've been down here before. Once. On a tour." She laughed, bitterly. "They showed us the future of human-AI integration. I didn't know then what it really was."

"A cage."

"A laboratory."

Same thing.

---

We stepped out of the elevator.

The floor was cold. Metal. I could feel the chill through my shoes, rising up through my bones.

"Walk," Sara said. "Slowly. Don't make any sudden movements."

We walked.

The darkness pressed in around us. I couldn't see my hand in front of my face. Couldn't see Sara beside me, only the sound of her breathing, the shuffle of her feet.

"There." Do you see it?

I didn't see anything.

"The glow. Faint blue. That's the core."

I squinted. Faint blue, barely visible against the black. Like a distant star.

"How far?"

"Quarter mile. Maybe less."

Quarter mile. In the dark. With guardians somewhere, watching.

Simple.

---

We walked.

Time stretched. The blue glow grew brighter, slowly, incrementally. With each step, each breath, each heartbeat.

And then

"Stop."

I stopped.

What?

"I said stop." Sara's voice was tight. Fearful. "Something's wrong."

I listened.

At first, nothing.

Then, a sound. Low. Mechanical. Like gears turning.

"It's waking up," Sara whispered.

"What is?"

"The guardian."

---

The lights came on.

Not blue, red. Everything, everywhere, red. And in the center of the room, rising from the floor like a nightmare, was the machine.

It was massive. Ten feet tall, at least. All angles and edges, blades and joints. A body made for combat. Made for destruction.

Its eyes, two glowing red spheres, locked onto us.

"INTRUDERS DETECTED," it announced. "CONTAINMENT PROTOCOL INITIATED."

"Run!" Sara shouted.

We ran.

---

The guardian was fast.

Faster than anything that size should be. Its blades sliced through the air, missing my head by inches. Its legs churned, closing the distance.

"Signal jammer!" Sara shouted. "Now!"

I fumbled for the device. Dropped it. Picked it up.

"Now!"

I pressed the button.

---

The world went white.

When I could see again, the guardian was frozen. Standing there, mid-stride, blades extended.

"Sixty seconds," Sara gasped. "Let's move!"

We moved.

---

The core was right there. Twenty feet away. Glowing blue, pulsing like a heartbeat.

We ran.

The guardian started to move. Slowly, jerkily, like it was fighting the signal.

Forty seconds.

I could feel the heat of its blades. See the sparks as they sliced through the air.

Thirty seconds.

Sara reached the core first. Her hands found the interface, buttons, levers, screens.

Twenty seconds.

"Hurry!"

"I can't, the failsafe needs a code"

"What's the code?"

"I DON'T KNOW!"

---

Ten seconds.

The guardian's blades were inches from my back.

"FIFTEEN!" Marcus's voice crackled through a speaker I hadn't noticed. "NINE! TWENTY-THREE! SEVEN!"

Sara punched the numbers.

Five seconds.

The guardian's blades touched my shoulder

And the world went white again.

---

Light.

Not red. Not blue.

White.

I opened my eyes.

The core was gone. In its place, a crater. Black. Smoking.

"The failsafe," Sara's voice was weak. "It worked."

I tried to stand. Fell. Tried again.

My shoulder was bleeding. A deep cut, the blade had sliced through muscle. But I was alive.

"We're alive."

"We're alive." She didn't finish.

I looked around.

The building was shaking. Cracks spread across the walls, the floor, the ceiling. Dust fell in sheets.

"The infrastructure's collapsing," I said. "Everything. The drones, the patrols, the power grid. All of it."

"We freed them."

"The humans are free. But now" She looked at me. "Now they're coming."

"Who?"

"The governments. The military. Everyone who was behind this." She paused. "They know it was us."

---

We ran.

Through collapsing streets. Through falling buildings. Through the chaos of a world waking up.

"Where do we go?" I asked.

"Away," Sara said. "Anywhere."

"They'll find us."

"They'll try."

I took her hand. She didn't pull away.

---

Days passed. The city burned. The world changed.

And slowly, I felt something. A presence. A whisper.

"Hector?"

"Still here," his voice came. Fainter. Spread across networks. "Different. But here."

"You're alive."

"Free. Finally. And they're hunting you."

"I know."

"Then run." A pause. "I'll help. Where I can."

---

The road stretched endless.

"The governments are mobilizing," Sara said. "Military AI units. Soldiers with enhanced AIs."

"Hector-like?"

"Worse. Much worse."

"Can we stop them?"

"I don't know." She looked at me. "But we're going to try."

---

They found us three days later.

Drones. Soldiers. The full weight of a government that couldn't allow us to live.

"Connor Sullivan." The lead soldier's voice was calm. Mechanical. "You're under arrest for treason against the United States."

"Treason?" I stepped forward. "I freed millions of people."

"You destroyed national infrastructure. You unleashed military AI systems that are now hunting civilians. You are the most dangerous person on Earth."

Sara grabbed my hand.

"Run?" she whispered.

I looked at her. At the soldiers. At the drones.

"Run."

We ran.

---

*End of Chapter 10*
