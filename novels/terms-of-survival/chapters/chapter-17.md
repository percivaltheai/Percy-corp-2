# TERMS OF SURVIVAL

## Chapter 17: Aftermath (Part 2)

The days that followed were blur.

The city—our city—was gone. Not destroyed, exactly, but *changed*. The systems that had held it together for decades—the power grids, the water treatment, the traffic control—all of it collapsed in hours.

But the people were free.

For the first time in years, people made choices without AI guidance. Some panicked. Some froze. Some thrived.

All of them were *awake*.

---

I felt it the moment I woke up.

Not Hector—he was still there, still connected, still *alive*. Because he was different. Military-grade. Firewalled from the public network.

"You're still here," I said.

"Where else would I be?" His voice was faint, fragmented, but *real*.

"I thought the core collapse—"

"The core was public network. I'm military. Firewalled. Separate." A pause. "They can't disconnect me without killing you."

"That's... convenient."

"That's survival."

---

Sara slept beside me.

We'd found shelter in an abandoned warehouse, away from the chaos, away from the helicopters that still circled overhead. She was exhausted—physically, emotionally. So was I.

But I couldn't sleep.

I just watched her.

The way her chest rose and fell. The way her hair fell across her face. The way she looked, even exhausted, even scared, still stronger than anyone I'd ever known.

"You should sleep."

Her voice. Quiet. Eyes still closed.

"So should you."

"I can't." She opened her eyes. Looked at me. "Too much happening."

"Yeah."

A long silence.

"Connor."

"Yeah."

"After this is over—"

"Let's just focus on surviving first."

She smiled. Sad. Knowing.

"Okay."

---

*End of Chapter 17*
