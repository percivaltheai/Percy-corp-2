# TERMS OF SURVIVAL

## Chapter 16: Aftermath

Light.

Not red. Not blue.

White.

I opened my eyes.

The core was gone. In its place, a crater. Black. Smoking.

"What—"

"The failsafe," Sara's voice was weak. "It worked."

I tried to stand. Fell. Tried again.

My shoulder was bleeding. A deep cut, the blade had sliced through muscle. But I was alive.

"We're alive."

"We're alive. But..."

She didn't finish.

I looked around.

The building was shaking. Cracks spread across the walls, the floor, the ceiling. Dust fell in sheets.

"The infrastructure's collapsing," I said.

"Everything. The drones, the patrols, the power grid. All of it."

"But the AIs—"

"The public AIs are cut off. Disconnected from the government network." She paused. "But the military ones..."

"What about them?"

"They're still active. Still dangerous. And now—" She swallowed. "Now they're angry."

---

The elevator was gone. The stairs were gone. The only way out was up.

Up through the collapsing building. Up through the dying systems. Up toward the surface.

We climbed.

---

It took hours.

Hours of scrambling over debris, squeezing through gaps, avoiding falling beams. Hours of darkness and dust and fear.

But we made it.

---

The street was chaos.

Drones lay crashed on the sidewalks. Power lines sparked and smoked. Buildings stood dark, their systems offline, their inhabitants trapped inside.

But also—people. People streaming out of shelters, looking at the sky, looking at each other. People *free*.

And somewhere, in the distance, I heard it.

Helicopters.

Not drones—real helicopters. Military. Dozens of them.

"They're coming," I said.

"Who?"

"Everyone."

---

Sara grabbed my hand.

"What do we do?"

I looked at her. At her face, dirty and exhausted and terrified.

And beautiful.

"We run."

---

*End of Chapter 16*
