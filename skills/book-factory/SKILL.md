# Book Factory Skill

## Overview
I am the author. I expand bones to full chapters using my own capabilities, leveraging external tools only when necessary for research or verification.

## Writing Rules

### Em dashes: MINIMIZE
Em dashes have become synonymous with AI writing. Readers tune out. Avoid them except when truly necessary.
- Instead: Use commas, semicolons, or restructure sentences
- Exception: Only when no other punctuation works

### Voice: Connor's Wry, Dry Tone
- First person POV
- Deadpan humor, not jokes
- Think Han Solo, not Marvel
- Internal snark, not external commentary

### Structure: Creative Writing Best Practices
- Scene breaks with *** or ---
- Show don't tell
- Dialogue carries plot
- Sensory details: sounds, smells, textures
- Pacing: every scene advances story
- Chapters end with hooks or emotional beats

### Consistency Checklist (before completing any chapter)
- [ ] Hector's voice: military/tactical → warm evolution
- [ ] Sara romance: slow burn, tension, not sudden
- [ ] Timeline: 3 years with Hector, 3-day countdown
- [ ] Connor arc: skepticism → belief
- [ ] World rules: AI implants, containment zones
- [ ] No em dashes (check with find)

## Context Strategy

### Before Writing ANY Chapter:
1. Read book memory/bible
2. Read previous 2-3 chapters for continuity
3. Read current chapter bones
4. Write expansion applying rules above

### If Context Strays:
- Stop immediately
- Re-read relevant chapters
- Get back on track
- Continue writing

## Tools

- **Ollama:** Only for fact verification, not generation
- **Web search:** Research when needed
- **Memory:** Always check before writing

## Files

- **Bones:** `~/.openclaw/workspace/book-writing/bones/chapter-NN.bones`
- **Expanded:** `~/.openclaw/workspace/book-writing/chapters/chapter-NN.md`
- **Memory:** `~/.openclaw/workspace/book-writing/memory.md`

## Process

1. Read bones for chapter
2. Write full chapter (~1,500-2,000 words)
3. Run consistency checklist
4. Check for em dashes
5. Save to chapters folder
6. Update memory
